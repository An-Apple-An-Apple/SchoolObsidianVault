**Identify two tones Bradbury employs and illustrate with specific phrases or passages. How does Bradbury’s tone influence the reader’s interpretation of the characters’ situation and the broader commentary on their sense of happiness and purpose?**
Bradbury's *The Vacation* begins with a ethereal description of the new world without the population. Bradbury uses vivid imagery to contribute to a grandiose scheme of the world with a clear focus on the rejuvenation of nature. Although the imagery of nature is often related to peace and growth, Bradbury contrasts this with subtle reminiscent of the past. Bradbury uses these juxtaposing tones to invite the audience to reflect on the realities of the disappearance of population and the freedom and isolation of such world. The family in *The Vacation* originally enjoys the disappearance of people in the world however subtle tone of reminiscent slowly build up. The implication of this highlights the contrast between reality of freedom and the emptiness of isolation and asks the audience to question the difference between expectation and realization. Near the end, reminiscence turns into a harsh solemn tone. Bradbury uses this solemn tone in "A flake of rust fell. A flower nodded. The sea was very loud." in to reveal the importance of human connection and the emptiness and bleakness that the disappearance of humanity.
