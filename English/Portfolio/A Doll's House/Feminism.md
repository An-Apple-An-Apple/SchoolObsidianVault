# Feminism
Could connect to lady Macbeth vs. Macbeth.

**Patriarchy**
- *Feminist theorist critique patriarchy as root of gender inequality*
- Men hold primary power
- Dominate male social roles
- **Structural Power**
- **Ideological Control**
- **Intersectionality**

**Separate Spheres**
- *Feminist critique separate spheres as patriarchal construct, limits women's freedom, perpetuates inequality*
- gender roles and separate expectation
- Public Sphere
	- Men encompass politics, law, commence
- Private Sphere
	- Women encompass domestic responsibility

**Gender Roles**
- *Feminist critique these roles as patriarchal structures*
- gender roles dictate what is "appropriate"
- Media and society perpetuate gender norms, shapes cultural expectations

**Toys**
Early 1920
- gender neutral clothing
WWII
- blue for girls, and pink for boys
1980
- blue for boys, and pink for girls

**Pink Tax**
- female-marketed products are more expensive

**Objectification**
- Treatment of women as objects

**Misogyny**
- prejudice against women in literature and culture
- through objectification, and perpetuation of gender roles

**Oppression**
- structurally, embedded in social system: wage gap, limited political representation
- culturally, through norms, stereotypes, and media

# Essentialism
People have innate essence that define them.
Feminism critique that essentialism uses natural essence to reinforce stereotypes.

# Infantialization
Treatment of women as children.
Lack of freedom, highlighting dependency.
Feminism crituque the infantialization perpetuates stereotypes, and justify inequality

# Sisterhood
Women have shared experience therefore can unite to to challenge patriarchy.
