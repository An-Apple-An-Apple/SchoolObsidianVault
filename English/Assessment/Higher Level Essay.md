# What is Higher Level Essay
1. answering a line of inquiry (LOI) 
2. focus on authorial choices
3. 1200-1500 words
4. 20% markings
5. Literary or non-literary 
# Scoring
## Criterion A: Knowledge, [Understanding], and Interpretation
1. **How well does the candidate demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the work or text chosen?**
	- What is the [message] of the [author]?
2. **To what extent does the candidate make use of knowledge and understanding of the work or text to draw conclusions in relation to the chosen topic?**
	- What are the [implication] of the [message]?
	- How does the [implications] of the [message] relate to the [LOI]?
3. **How well are ideas supported by references to the work or text in relation to the chosen topic?**
	- How intricate is the [in-text citations] used to support the [message]?
## Criterion B: Analysis and Evaluation ([Authorial Choice])
1. **To what extent does the candidate analyze and evaluate how the choices of language, technique and style, and broader authorial choices shape meaning in relation to the chosen topic?**
	- How does [authorial choice] impact the [readers]?
	- How does the [authorial choice] support the [main idea]?
## Criterion C: [Focus] and Organization 
1. **How well organized, [focused] and developed is the presentation of ideas in the essay?**
	- How easy is the essay to [follow]?
	- How [focused] is the essay on the [LOI]?
2. **How well are examples integrated into the essay?**
## Criterion D: Language ([Appropriateness])
1. **How [clear, varied and accurate] is the language?**
	- How clear [expression] of [ideas]?
	- How [varied] are the [sentence structures]?
	- How [accurate] is the [language]?
2. **How [appropriate] is the choice of register and style? 
	- How [appropriate] is the language, tone and sentence structure?
