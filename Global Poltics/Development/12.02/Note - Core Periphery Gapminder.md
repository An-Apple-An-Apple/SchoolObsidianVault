Core/Periphery (IB perfered)
- a reference of globalization
- Core: More globalized
	- Old Core
		- "Have a head start"
		- globalization
		- industrialization
		- power (hard political, hard finance, structural)
		- interdependence
		- ie. Canada, Germany, USA
	- New Core
		- "New members"
		- advances in political and human rights
		- increasing globalization
		- regional power
		- reliant on old core for capita or trade
		- ie. Malaysia, Greece, China
- Periphery: Less Globalized
	- Near-core
		- growing but potential to join
		- substantial gap between rich/poor and rural/urban
		- subsistance agricultural
			- farming for your family
		- transitional economies 
		- dependency on core states
		- ie. Turkey, South Africa, Thailand
	- Far Periphery
		- heavily dependent on core and semi-periphery for trade, investment, and technology
		- limited industrialization
		- largely subsistence agricultural
		- limited tax base
		- limited infrastructural development
		- ie. sub-saharan African countries, parts of South Asia
- can shift
- development and dependency theory
- power, inequality, interdependence
